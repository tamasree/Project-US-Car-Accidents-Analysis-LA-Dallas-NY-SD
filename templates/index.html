Project Summary



Motivation

Reducing traffic accidents is an essential public safety challenge all over the world., therefore accident analysis is subject of much research. Since we had the data up till 2020, our team was curious to analyze considering 2020 was global lockdown period.


The purpose of this analysis to answer the following questions:


  • Which states and cities of the 4 have most traffic accidents?
  • What is the time of the day, the day of the week and the month with the higher number of accidents?
  • How many accidents and how they fluctuate over two years?
  • What are the most common weather conditions on the days of the accidents?
  • How many accidents have a severity level of 1, 2, 3 and 4? and what could be the factors?


About the data

Data source:


  • We used the data from Kaggle.com . The data set found in Kaggle covers for 49 states, from the years 2016 to 2020. However our team concentrated on the data of past 2 years due to huge volume.
  • Source : https://www.kaggle.com/sobhanmoosavi/us-accidents

Data Exploration:


The dataset is downloaded as CSV format, and has 47 columns totaly in this dataset. There are about 1.5 million rocords of accidents across USA and territories that make this dataset very larg (569.14 MB). We dropped unrelated columns and the remaining columns are: "ID", "Severity", "Start_Time", "End_Time", "Start_Lat", "Start_Lng", "Street", "City", "County", "State", "Wind_Speed(mph)", "Precipitation(in)", "Weather_Condition", "Amenity", "Amenity", "Crossing", "Give_Way", "Junction", "No_Exit", "Railway", "Roundabout", "Station Stop", "Traffic_Calming", "Traffic_Signal", "Turning_Loop"


Data Processing (ETL):


  • Extracted the data from raw csv file. Used pandas to complete ETL Process.
  • Identified Null values
  • Changed the datatype of important columns (Date/Time)
  • Filtered the data to select cities
  • Load the clean dataframe to csv for the next step

Visualization:

  • Visualization: Consists of two pages for general analysis and geographical analysis
  • API: This page holds queries in Json files grenerated from Flask.
  • About Us: A page for team members introduction


Conclusion

Findings

After the analysis, we found the following:

  • Los Angeles has the highest accidents count across four seclected cities.
  • Mondays and Fridays between 12 to 6pm has most count. December has seen highest rate.
  • Surprisingly 2020 tops the charts, considering it was the pandemic year with lockdown.
  • Most accidents were caused on a moderately fair day. Also, most accidents happened at junction and traffic signals.
  • Most Accidents have severity level -2 which means there were minor injuries, 4 being the fatal.
